Interview Experience @ Capgemini

Capgemini - Computer Engineering

Round 1 - Online Aptitude Test (via CoCubes):
This round included three sections:

Technical Aptitude:
- Pseudocode (15 questions)
- Algorithms (10 questions)
Covered basics of Trees, Graphs, Stacks, Queues, Linked Lists, loops, bitwise operations, etc.
Note: This was an elimination round.

English Aptitude:
Simple questions on synonyms, antonyms, grammar, and comprehension.
Note: Also elimination-based.

Game-Based Assessment:
A 27-minute game test with 4 parts:
- Deductive Thinking
- Inductive Thinking
- Grid Challenge (recall and sequence memory)
- Motion Challenge (push block to goal - I cleared 15 levels)

Afterwards, a Behavioral Competency Test was conducted but didn't confirm progression.

Round 2 - Coding Round:
Based on the job role, I was not eligible for the coding round and was directly promoted to the technical round.

Round 3 - Technical Interview (via Superset):
The interviewer asked me about:
- Family background and academic journey
- My final year project
- Favorite technologies and preferred coding languages
- Why Capgemini and not higher studies

Confidence and clarity were key to clearing this round. Most of us cleared this round.

Round 4 - HR Interview:
A casual and personality-based round. Questions included:
- Hobbies and ambitions
- Happiest moment of life
- A moment I was proud of
- Willingness to relocate
- Agreement on the 2-year service bond

Tips:
Be confident and curious. Use the interview opportunity to ask thoughtful questions to show interest.

Result:
Selected at Capgemini.
