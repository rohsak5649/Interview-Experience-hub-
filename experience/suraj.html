Interview Experience @ Goldman Sachs

Goldman Sachs - Analyst Role

Round 1: Online Assessment
Total: 7 sections, 70 questions
Marking: +5 for correct, -2 for wrong
Sections included:

  • Abstract Reasoning – Pattern recognition in shapes
  • Verbal Ability – Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension
  • Numerical Reasoning – Word problems, percentages, ratios
  • Logical Reasoning – Puzzles, sequences, arrangements
  • Numerical Calculations – Fast arithmetic
  • Data Interpretation – Graphs, charts, tables
  • Diagrammatic Reasoning – Flowcharts, logical diagrams

Round 2: Online Assessment
1. Coding Section: 3 DSA questions (Medium to Hard). No negative marking. Topics included:

  • Arrays
  • Trees
  • Graphs
  • Dynamic Programming
2. HR-Based Essay: Topics like: “What will you do if one of your teammates cannot complete their work? How will you handle work pressure and meet deadlines?” 3. Advanced MCQs: 10 Questions
Marking: +5 for correct, -2 for wrong
Topics: OS, DBMS, CN, and other advanced CS concepts

Round 3: Online Technical Interview

  • Self-introduction
  • Project and internship discussion
  • Live coding: Check if two strings are anagrams + optimization
  • Technical questions: Stack, Queue, DBMS

Round 4: Online Coding Interview
Question:
Given a list of strings and a string of keywords (space-separated), print all strings that contain any of the keywords.
Example: 

strings = [
  "The weather is sunny today",
  "I love playing football",
  "Python is a powerful language",
  "Let's go for a run"
]
keywords = "python football run"
Output: 
I love playing football
Python is a powerful language
Let's go for a run

Round 5: Technical + Managerial Interview

  • Introduction and detailed project discussion
  • Internship experience
  • Real-world logic-based scenarios
  • Behavioral questions like: “What will you do if your teammate gets all the credit for your efforts?”
Focus was on attitude, teamwork, and conflict handling.

Result: Selected at Goldman Sachs
